100-point scoring model

Every lead is scored across four signal groups. Transparent, explainable, no black-box AI.

35 pts
Distress / Motivation

Pre-foreclosure and foreclosure max the group (35 pts). Tax-delinquent gets 30, probate 25, vacant 20, code violations 10. Bonus points for stacked signals in notes.

30 pts
Equity / Discount

40%+ below ARV earns full 30 pts. 30% = 20 pts, 20% = 10 pts. If no ARV, uses MAO: asking ≤ MAO earns 30 pts, within 10% of MAO earns 15.

20 pts
Lead Age (DOM proxy)

Leads 90+ days old score 20 pts — sellers that old are usually motivated to move. 60+ days = 15, 30+ = 10, 7+ = 5. Fresh leads score 2.

10 pts
Contact Completeness

Email + phone = 10 pts. Either one = 5 pts. No contact info = 0. Prevents high-equity leads with no way to reach the seller from polluting your call list.

5 pts
Pipeline Engagement

Leads already in "contacted" or "negotiating" status get a 5-point bump — you're already in conversation, so bump them up the call list.

Questions

What if I have fewer than 50 leads?＋

It still works — and it's arguably more useful with a small list because every lead decision matters more. The agent scores all leads you have, regardless of count. With a small list, the hot-list might be 2–3 leads, but those are your best bets.

How does Lead Sieve connect to my leads?＋

Lead Sieve reads from the same flat-JSON lead stores that all our other agents write to (data/leads.json, data/hd_leads.json, data/ps_leads.json). If you're using HUDScout, PropScout, or the OAS Outreach agent, your leads are already in the right format.

What if a lead is scored HOT but I've already contacted them?＋

Leads with a "contacted" or "negotiating" status get an engagement bonus (5 pts) — they'll already appear near the top of WARM/HOT. The auto-trigger only fires on leads with status "new" and no prior follow-up, so you won't get duplicate outreach on leads already in conversation.

Can I adjust the scoring weights?＋

Annual subscribers get 2 customization slots per year. Email us which group you want to reweight and we'll adjust the model for your market (e.g., if probate is unusually profitable in your area, we can raise its distress score above the default 25 pts).
