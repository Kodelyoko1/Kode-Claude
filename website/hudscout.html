Background

Why HUD homes are different from regular REOs.

🏠

Government-owned

HUD homes are former FHA-insured properties that were foreclosed on. HUD takes title and lists them publicly through its HUD Home Store website — no agent, no negotiation, just a public bid process.

📅

Exclusive bid window

New listings enter a 15-day “owner-occupant” period, then open to investors. The earliest you bid in the investor window, the better your odds — competition thins fast after the first week.

💰

Often below ARV

HUD sets list price based on an appraisal, not market dynamics. In softer markets or on properties needing significant repairs, the list price is frequently 20–40% below ARV — leaving real wholesale margin.

🔍

Hard to monitor manually

HUD Home Store doesn’t send alerts. You have to log in and search your markets manually — or miss listings entirely. That’s exactly what HUDScout automates.

How HUDScout works

Daily scan. Instant alert. Deal in your pipeline.

Step 1 — Daily

Scans HUD Home Store

Uses the same JSON endpoint HUD’s own website uses — not screen-scraping, not fragile. Sweeps every state you configure for new listings, price changes, and status updates.

Step 2 — Deduped

Normalizes and dedupes

Each listing is keyed by HUD case number. Re-runs only report truly new properties — you won’t get the same address twice across separate scans.

Step 3 — Digest

Sends you a deal digest

A clean markdown report lands in your inbox: property address, case number, list price, bed/bath, sqft, status (bidding open/in-offer/sold), and a direct link to the listing.

Step 4 — Pipeline

Feeds your deal pipeline

Every HUD lead is added to data/leads.json as a LEAD-NNNN record with lead_source: HUDScout — the Deal Analyzer picks them up automatically on the next batch run.

FAQ

Honest answers.

What states does HUDScout cover?

Any state(s) you configure in your HD_STATES environment variable. Comma-separated postal codes: e.g. ME,NH,VT,MA. HUD Home Store covers all 50 states plus DC and territories.

How often does it scan?

Daily. You can run it more frequently by adjusting your cron, but HUD typically updates listings once per day. Running more often than daily won’t find more deals — it’ll just send you the same digest twice.

Can I bid on HUD homes as an investor?

Yes — after the 15-day owner-occupant period, the listing opens to all bidders including investors. You’ll need to work with a HUD-registered real estate agent to place bids. HUDScout tells you which listings are in the investor window vs. owner-occupant only.

Does this work with the Deal Analyzer?

Yes — that’s a key feature. HUD leads are automatically added to data/leads.json with lead_source: HUDScout. When you run the Deal Analyzer in batch mode (--auto), it picks up those leads automatically and runs the numbers.

What happens if HUD changes their API?

HUD occasionally rotates their antiforgery token flow. When that happens, we patch the scraper within 24–48 hours and push an update. Monthly subscribers get update notifications by email.
