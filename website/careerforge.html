The problem

Generic resumes fail — even for qualified candidates.

ATS filters first

Most companies run applications through Applicant Tracking Systems before a human sees them. ATS systems match keywords — if your resume doesn’t use the exact phrases from the job posting, it’s filtered out.

⏳

6-second human scan

If it gets past the ATS, a recruiter skims for 6 seconds on average. They’re looking for title match, skill match, and company names. Generic resumes don’t pass this scan.

📄

Tailoring is time-consuming

Doing it right means reading the JD thoroughly, identifying required vs preferred skills, rewriting bullet points, and adjusting your summary — for every single application. Most people just don’t.

FAQ

Honest answers.

Will you add fake experience to my resume?

No — never. CareerForge only reframes the experience you already have. What changes is the language, order, and framing — not the facts. The gap analysis tells you what’s missing so you can decide whether to develop those skills, not fabricate them.

What format will the resume be delivered in?

Markdown and plain text by default — both are ATS-safe. If you need a Word or PDF version, let us know and we’ll convert it. Most ATS systems actually parse plain text more accurately than formatted PDFs anyway.

What industries do you cover?

Any industry. The agent works on the JD text regardless of whether it’s a software engineer role, a nurse, a sales director, or a warehouse manager. It reads what the posting emphasizes and rewrites your resume to match those emphases.
