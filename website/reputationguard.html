FAQ

Common questions

What platforms do you monitor?
Currently Google Business Profile and Yelp. Both use saved HTML snapshots — you navigate to your listing, save the page, and drop the file in data/rg_snapshots/. No browser extension, no API credentials, no OAuth flow needed. Additional platforms (TripAdvisor, Facebook) are on the roadmap.
Do you need my Google Business login?
No. ReputationGuard works entirely from publicly visible HTML snapshots of your listing. You never share login credentials, OAuth tokens, or API keys. The agent reads the same page a customer sees — just automated and parsed into a structured digest.
How quickly do I get notified of a new negative review?
Notification speed depends on how frequently you drop new snapshots. With the recommended weekly scan cadence, you'll know about any new 1–3 star review within 7 days. Drop snapshots more frequently (daily or every few days) and turnaround shortens accordingly — the agent runs on every new file it finds in the input directory.
What is "acquisition targeting" and who is it for?
The Deep Audit scans competitor or market listings and scores each business by negative review density, rating decline trend, and complaint frequency. Businesses with consistently poor ratings and unresolved complaints often signal operational or ownership distress — making them strong candidates for wholesale acquisition outreach. This feature is designed for real estate wholesalers, business brokers, and investors scouting motivated sellers in the local business market.
