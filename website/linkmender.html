Questions

What is a "resource page"?＋

A resource page is a curated list of links that a blogger, educator, or organization maintains to help their audience find useful tools, guides, and references. They typically have titles like "Recommended Resources," "Helpful Links," or "Best Tools for [Niche]." Because they're maintained by humans and link to third-party sites, they accumulate dead links over time — which is exactly the opportunity LinkMender exploits for your SEO strategy.

How many links get scanned in an audit?＋

A single-page audit typically scans 20–150 links depending on how densely the resource page is populated. The $47/mo monitoring tier covers up to 10 resource pages per weekly cycle, which means up to 1,500 link checks per week. The $197 Agency Lead List surfaces 100+ resource pages and pre-identifies the dead links across all of them — so you're not starting from scratch.

Do I need to do any technical SEO myself?＋

No. The entire discovery-and-scan pipeline runs automatically. Your only job is to send the outreach emails we draft for you and make sure your replacement page exists and is live before you reach out. If you're on the monthly monitoring plan, you'll receive an email alert each time a new dead link is found — with the outreach draft already attached.

What niche can I target?＋

Any niche with a meaningful web presence works well — real estate, personal finance, health & wellness, SaaS tools, digital marketing, education, travel, and more. When you order, simply tell us your niche and your target URL (the page you want curators to link to). The search dorks are customized to your vertical before the scan runs.
